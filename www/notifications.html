Following severe flooding in Johannesburg on Wednesday night, 9 November, the South African Weather Service has issued a warning of more heavy rains underway for Gauteng on Thursday, 18 May.

Gauteng Weather tweeted saying the heavy rains, severe thunderstorms and hail can be expected between 12:00 and 23:00 on Thursday.

The SA Weather Service warns that thunderstoms will spread to places over the central and eastern Free State, eastern parts of the North West, Mpumalanga highveld, Gauteng and southern parts of Limpopo on Thursday.

The rain in Gauteng is expected to carry through to Friday.

White maize futures rose by the daily limit for a second day as dry weather and forecasts for two weeks without rain raised concern about the strength of a recovery from last year’s poor harvest.

The country’s prime corn-growing areas in the Free State and Mpumalanga provinces had strong rain in November, following the worst drought since records started in 1904 last year.

The dry weather has returned to the Free State this month, Brink van Wyk, a trader at BVG, said by phone.

White corn for delivery in March climbed by as much as R100, to R3 895 a metric ton by 9:38 in Johannesburg. The most actively traded contract touched a low this year of R3 428 on October 10.
